What Is Acupuncture?

Acupuncture is a healing therapy originating from Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) over 2,500 years ago. It involves inserting fine sterile needles into specific points on the body to balance energy flow and promote natural healing.

Today, acupuncture is practiced worldwide and is increasingly integrated into modern healthcare for pain management, stress reduction, and overall wellness.

How Does Acupuncture Work?

Traditional Chinese Medicine: Acupuncture helps restore balance to the body's vital energy (Qi) by stimulating specific points along energy pathways called meridians.

Modern Scientific View: Research suggests acupuncture stimulates nerves, muscles, and connective tissues while encouraging the release of natural pain-relieving chemicals such as endorphins.

Safety & Treatment

Safety, Precautions, and What to Expect

Safety & Precautions

  • Performed using sterile, single-use needles
  • Generally safe when administered by trained professionals
  • Minor soreness or bruising may occasionally occur
  • Pregnant individuals should inform the practitioner before treatment
  • Patients with pacemakers should discuss electroacupuncture use with their healthcare provider

What to Expect During Treatment

  • Initial health consultation
  • Personalized treatment planning
  • Comfortable needle placement with minimal discomfort
  • Session duration typically 20–60 minutes
  • Multiple sessions may be recommended for chronic conditions
