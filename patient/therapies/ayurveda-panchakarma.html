What is Panchakarma?

Panchakarma, meaning “five actions” in Sanskrit, is Ayurveda’s core detoxification therapy. It uses five therapeutic procedures to eliminate toxins and restore balance to the doshas. More than a simple detox, it targets deep-seated impurities caused by digestion and lifestyle, promoting holistic healing for sustained well-being.

The Science of Ama

Ama is described in Ayurveda as a toxic residue that forms due to incomplete digestion and sluggish metabolism. It can clog body channels, disturb digestion, and reduce vitality.

  • Coated tongue
  • Heavy limbs and lethargy
  • Bloating or constipation
  • Chronic fatigue
  • Mental fogginess

Preparation

Preparing for Panchakarma: Purvakarma

Purvakarma is the pre-cleansing stage. It prepares the body by loosening toxins and moving them toward the digestive tract for safe elimination.

Oleation

Warm medicated oils are applied externally or used internally to lubricate channels and soften tissues.

Fomentation

Herbal steam therapy helps open pores and liquefy impurities for easier elimination.

Five Actions

The Five Core Panchakarma Therapies

Vamana

Therapeutic vomiting to expel excess Kapha.

Virechana

Controlled purgation to remove Pitta toxins.

Basti

Medicated enemas to cleanse the colon and balance Vata.

Nasya

Nasal administration of oils for sinus and head wellness.

Raktamokshana

Blood purification therapy under expert supervision.
