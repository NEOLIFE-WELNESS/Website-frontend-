What Is Chiropractic Therapy?

Chiropractic therapy is a natural and non-invasive system of care that focuses on the relationship between the spine, joints, muscles, nerves, posture, and overall body movement.

The goal is to identify movement restrictions, postural imbalance, and musculoskeletal stress that may contribute to pain, stiffness, reduced mobility, and physical discomfort.

The Philosophy Behind Chiropractic Care

Chiropractic care recognises that the spine and musculoskeletal system play an important role in posture, movement, balance, and daily comfort. When joints or surrounding muscles become restricted or strained, it may affect mobility and create discomfort.

Care is planned according to the patient’s symptoms, physical condition, posture, medical history, and movement patterns.

Spinal Alignment

Focuses on improving spinal movement, posture, and body alignment through carefully selected manual techniques.

Joint Mobility

Supports smoother joint movement and helps reduce stiffness caused by physical strain, poor posture, or limited activity.

Muscle Relaxation

Helps reduce muscular tension and supports better coordination between muscles, joints, and the nervous system.

Manual Care

Core Chiropractic Techniques

Spinal Manipulation

Controlled manual movements may be used to improve mobility in restricted spinal joints and support better alignment and movement.

Joint Mobilisation

Gentle, repetitive movements may be used to improve the flexibility and function of stiff joints.

Soft Tissue Techniques

Selected techniques may help relax tight muscles, reduce physical tension, and improve local circulation.

Posture Correction

Guidance may be provided to improve sitting, standing, sleeping, and working posture to reduce repeated strain.

Stretching and Movement Guidance

Personalized exercises and stretches may be recommended to support flexibility, strength, balance, and long-term mobility.

Your Session

What Happens During a Chiropractic Session?

Health Assessment

The practitioner discusses your symptoms, lifestyle, physical activity, health history, and current concerns.

Posture and Movement Check

Posture, flexibility, joint movement, and areas of physical tension may be assessed.

Personalized Care

Suitable manual techniques, mobilisation, stretching, and lifestyle guidance are selected according to individual needs.

Follow-Up Guidance

Patients may receive advice on posture, stretching, physical activity, and preventive care.

Chiropractic Care and Lifestyle

  • Healthy sleeping positions
  • Balanced physical activity
  • Weight and lifestyle management
  • Stress reduction practices

Who Should Consult Before Treatment?

Patients with fractures, severe osteoporosis, recent surgery, spinal infection, advanced neurological symptoms, cancer affecting bones, or serious medical conditions should inform the practitioner and obtain appropriate medical evaluation before treatment.

Urgent medical care: Seek immediate care for severe pain, loss of sensation, sudden weakness, difficulty walking, loss of bladder or bowel control, or symptoms following major trauma.
